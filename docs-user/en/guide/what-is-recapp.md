# What is Recapp?

Recapp is a collaborative quiz tool for university teaching. Unlike conventional quiz tools where the teacher writes all the questions, Recapp puts question-writing in the hands of students — then has everyone answer each other's questions. This turns revision into an active, collaborative exercise.

## How a typical session works

A Recapp quiz usually unfolds across two course meetings:

1. **End of session 1** — The teacher opens a quiz in editing mode and shares the link or QR code. Students write questions about the day's content directly in Recapp.
2. **Between sessions** — The teacher reviews the submitted questions, makes edits where needed, and decides which ones to include.
3. **Start of session 2** — The teacher starts the quiz. Everyone answers the questions. Results appear live in the statistics view and can be discussed as a group.

This approach means students engage with the material twice: once when writing a question (which requires deeper understanding than simply answering one), and once when working through the full set.

## Roles

**Teachers** create and manage quizzes, curate student-submitted questions, control the quiz lifecycle, and review statistics. A quiz can have multiple teachers — for example, a lecturer and a tutor sharing the same quiz.

**Students** (referred to as *participants* in Recapp) join quizzes via a shared link or QR code, write questions during the editing phase, and answer questions once the teacher starts the quiz.

## Access

At the University of Göttingen, Recapp is available to all university members at [https://recapp.uni-goettingen.de](https://recapp.uni-goettingen.de). Log in with your standard university account — no separate registration is needed.

Recapp is open source and can be self-hosted by any institution with the appropriate server infrastructure.
