# Creating Questions <Badge type="info" text="Students" />

When the quiz is in **editing mode**, you can submit questions that your fellow students will later answer. Writing a question is the core of how Recapp works — it asks you to think carefully about the course material and formulate something meaningful.

## Adding a question

In the **Quiz questions** tab, click **+ New question**.

**1. Choose how your name appears**  
Select anonymous, pseudonym, or real name — the available options depend on what your teacher has set. Your teacher will always see who submitted a question, but other students will not.

**2. Select a question type**  
Only types your teacher has enabled are available:
- **Free text** — respondents write their own answer; not automatically graded
- **Single choice** — one correct answer among several options
- **Multiple choice** — one or more answers can be correct

**3. Write your question**  
Type in the question editor. The editor supports basic markdown — use the toolbar buttons for bold, italic, lists, and links, or click **?** for the full syntax reference.

**4. Add answer options** (for single- and multiple-choice questions)  
Click **Add answer** for each option. Tick the checkbox next to each correct answer. Use the arrows to reorder options and the minus button to remove one.

**5. Add a hint** (optional)  
Use the hint field to give respondents a helpful pointer about how to read the question.

**6. Save**  
Click **Save question**. Your question appears in the list.

## Editing your question

You can edit your own questions as long as the quiz is still in editing mode. Click the **pencil icon** on your question card, make your changes, and click **Save question**.

Once your teacher switches to curation mode, editing is no longer possible.

## What happens next

After you submit questions, your teacher reviews them. They may edit, hide, or reorder them before starting the quiz. You can see your own questions in the list — but not other students' questions. You'll encounter those when the quiz starts.

## Comments

If your teacher has enabled comments, you can leave a comment on the question you're currently answering during the started phase. See [Answering a Quiz](./answer-quiz#comments) for details.
